My Journey Through the World of Art

Drawing is one of the skills in the world where you can't see your path ahead as in, learning to draw isn't a straight line. Depending on what you want to learn, it could take a complete different approach.

If your goal is to learn to master drawing and painting by the eye, then repetitively doing still life drawing with perspective techniques will benefit you the most. On the other hand, if your goal is to learn to draw things by imagination, the earlier mentioned practice is not optimal. As for imagination drawing practice, you should train yourself to rotate and see things in 3d space. Practicing simplifying objects as basic shapes will help you draw from imagination extremely well. For example, the torso of a human is shaped similarly to a pillow with certain curves, and the hip is shaped similarly to a basket.

I personally chose to pursue imaginative drawing, mostly concentrated in the human body in anime style art. A learning strategy that helped me develop in this category was looking at professional artist's layers and seeing how they structured their drawings from basic sketch to finished fully painted characters.

Important Things When Starting Out

In my opinion, The number one most important thing to have is an experienced artist as a resource. Most of the problems you will encounter will be things that vary from person to person, so having professional guidance is a very useful tool.
